How to Use

Pick Your Filters:

Using the dropdown menu select what category you want to open. A sub-menu will display buttons for all filters you wish to include. For instance, to get a random video game either on the PS3 or PS2 with a metacritic score between 80-89% click both the "PS3" and the "PS2" buttons from the "Platform" button list and then from the "Score" list click the "80-89%" button. Note that filters are cumulative and selecting both "PS3" and "PS2" buttons means you have a chance to randomly get games from either system. Running the search with no filters selected will give you a completely random game.

Run the Search:

Navigate to the section of the page labeled "Search" and click the run button located on the fixed website footer. Note you can click the run anytime but you can only view the results if you navigate to the appropriate section.

View your Results:

Information on your selected game is displayed, with the final row of information being a series of download links or Amazon Links at which you can find the game (Hosted by Emuparadise). The program runs a search on emuparadise first, if links are found they will be displayed, if not an Amazon search is run to display Amazon links.

Q&A:
  • Q: What's up with the title screen? The games keep changing!
  • A: Yes! The game images on the title screen are completely random each time you refresh the website. Take your time and enjoy refreshing the page. Unfortunately these are just images and are not links to anything so don't try click on them.
  • Q: Is this site SFW (Safe for Work)?
  • A: No, there are over 50,000 games in the randomizer database including some NSFW video games so refresh the title page, and run the search at your own risk.
  • Q: Why doesn't the search present you with a list of video games specified by my filters?
  • A: Well that kind of defeats the purpose of being a "random" video game website. At that point it would just be a place where you can search for video games, and there are tons of other websites that already accomplish that task.
  • Q:Can we get at least 5 random video games with our filters?
  • A: No, I do not plan on implementing a feature like that anytime soon. However, I will consider adding a feature where all your search results are kept on the page. Or I might even consider a feature where all the video game search results are cached in your browsers local storage! Actually that sounds cool...
  • Q:Why itsn't ____ Platform on this list?
  • A: While the database does have a lot of platforms it is not completely exhaustive. There are some missing platforms such as arcade machines etc...
  • Q: Why does the release year stop at 2016? I know new games have come out in 2017!
  • A: Yes, I am aware of the new video game releases! This database/application is mainly for the use of finding obsure video games from the past 30 years and it is very comprehensive. Furthermore, the video games on this website will not be updated with the newest video game releases for this purpose.
  • Q: Where do did you get the rating data from?
  • A: The ratings from the games come from Metacritic. Many games do not have rating data because metacritic did not rate those games. (Too old etc...)
  • Q: Help! I can't seem to find any remasters!
  • A: Yeah the database does not include remasters as a separate video game. So if you are looking for Final Fantasy X-2 remaster, you won't find it.
  • Q: Some of these ratings are don't match on Metacritic...
  • A: Yes the way this site was programmed the most recent score from metacritic is added to the game. In some cases the the game is remastered and then the remaster metascore is used. (See above about remasters)
  • Q: Why do you choose to display Final Fantasy 7, Animal Crossing, Shin Megami Tensei etc... On the information part of the website?
  • A: Those are some of my favorite video games and I wanted to display the video games chronologically started from the left (Pong).
  • Q: Ughhh why DISQUS???
  • A: This application/site is just a small side project for me and I want to work on other things in my free time. I don't really want to build a custom comment system and DISQUS is so easy to install and manage. Try it, you might like it!
  • Q: Sometimes in the result screen _____ has nothing in it?
  • A: Yes, not all games have data in every field. If it's blank it was not in the database. The only sure thing that will never be blank is the Platforms field and the name.
  • Q: Where did you get the time to beat data?
  • A: The only place on the Internet that has data like this, HLTB.com. The data for these times to beat were taken around September 2016, so they probably will not match the data displayed on the actual site but they still give a good estimate of how long it should take you beat a game.
  • Q: Why aren't there download links for the other platforms?
  • A: There are NO download links for newer consoles, please if you have the means and can find a store copy of any game you found through the randomizer, please purchase to the support the community and developers of these games.
  • Q: What does "No rating data" and "No time data" mean?
  • A: It means when you press the filter button, games that do not have times to beat, or ratings are including the filter search.
  • Q: I've never heard of some of these games!
  • A: Yes, even games released exclusively in Japan and other regions are included.
  • Q: Can you bring back the Amazon Links for the games that do not have download links?
  • A: Of course, if there is enough demand (go to the comments people) then I will gladly add that feature back in.
  • Q: Does this work in every major browser?
  • A: Yes-ish, it has been tested in the most recent versions of Opera, Firefox, IE and Chrome. It works best in Chrome, but has not been tested in Safari.
  • Q: Is this responsive/mobile first?
  • A:Yes, I tested it on most mobile devices. There are media queries!
  • Q: Why do the download link sometimes show unrelated products/games?
  • A: The way the site works is the name of the game is search on emupardise or Amazon, that means my search is only as good as theirs so there will occasionally be errors.
